Andøya, Norway

Andøya is the 10th largest island in Norway, situated 300 kilometres (190 miles) north of the Arctic circle, in the Vesterålen archipelago.

Andøya is known for the numerous bog areas used for the extensive production of peat. Andøya is also well known for its fishing industry, and cloudberries. Besides the flat and largely continuous bog areas, Andøya also consists of steep mountain ranges reaching up to 700 metres (2,300 ft) high. The Andøya Space Center which was founded in 1962 is also located on the island. Since 1962, over 1,200 sounding rockets of all known configurations have been launched from this site.

It's climate is between the subarctic and the subpolar oceanic climates, and yet is extremely mild despite its latitude because of the Gulf Stream influence. Summers are cool due to the Gulf Stream moderation, whereas even during the polar night period (winter), daytime highs frequently rise above freezing.
